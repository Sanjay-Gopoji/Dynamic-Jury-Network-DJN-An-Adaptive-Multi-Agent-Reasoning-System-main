Dynamic Jury Network

An adaptive multi-agent reasoning system that mimics jury-style deliberation to enhance decision quality in AI-assisted problem solving.

How DJN Works

Multiple LLMs deliberate as jurors, coordinated by a Moderator/Judge to converge on reliable decisions.

🎯
Problem Statement

Single AI models often produce confident yet inconsistent or biased responses, especially for complex or ambiguous queries. There is a lack of structured mechanisms to validate, critique, and consolidate multiple AI opinions into a reliable outcome.

⚠️
Existing Solutions & Drawbacks

Existing AI systems typically rely on a single model or naive ensemble averaging. These approaches suffer from limited transparency, lack of debate, no consensus metrics, and an inability to explain disagreements between models.

Our Solution
💡
Proposed Solution

DJN introduces a structured multi-round deliberation framework where multiple LLMs independently reason, critique each other's responses, and iteratively converge toward a consensus under the supervision of a Moderator/Judge.

✨
Merits of DJN

✓ Improved decision reliability through consensus
✓ Transparent reasoning and disagreement tracking
✓ Adaptive juror selection based on performance
✓ Suitable for high-stakes decision support systems
